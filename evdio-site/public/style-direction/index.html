Every surface draws from these eight colors and their opacities. The page background is a lighter variant of Pantone 7506 C (#FAF3E0 / #FDFAF0). Brick red and gold appear only as sparse accents — a rule, a word in italic, a corner tile.

Typography — one family, two weights

Display — Instrument Serif

Come home.

Large display headlines, masthead, pull quotes. Thin, optical-size-aware serif with warmth and presence.

Display Italic — Instrument Serif Italic

A grace-filled life.

Accent words inside headlines (in brick red). Pull quotes and scripture citations.

UI / Label — Instrument Sans 600

IN THIS ISSUE

Eyebrows, kickers, navigation, buttons, captions. Tightly tracked uppercase for editorial labels.

Body — Instrument Sans 400 / 500

Every parish in the Diocese of Evansville has a priest or staff member ready to answer your questions.

Long-form reading, interface copy, footer text. A clean humanist sans-serif designed to pair with its serif sibling.

Photography — real diocesan imagery, no stock

Every photograph in these mockups comes from the diocesan photo library (Google Drive) or The Message's published archive. No AI-generated imagery. Swap any photo for another from the library and the layout holds.

Accessibility — built to AAA

  • Body text on cream uses navy (#0A2240) — passes WCAG AAA at all sizes.
  • Body text on navy uses cream (#EEDAB3) — passes AAA.
  • Brick red (#A44637) on cream passes AAA. Dark gold (#D09000) used only for thin rules and italic accents, never small body text.
  • Visible focus states: 2px bright-gold outline with 3px offset on every interactive element.
  • Semantic landmarks, breadcrumb, skip link, proper heading hierarchy, alt text on every image.

What the client is choosing

  • Direction A — the editorial register for the homepage, About, Our Bishop, and news-driven landings.
  • Direction B — the long-read register for sacraments, prayer & worship, and any page that asks the reader to stay a while.
  • Most likely outcome: both directions live together. A for arrival and brand moments, B for depth.
