Style sheet · Curves

The curves system.

Rounded corners, pill buttons, circular numerals, arch-topped photos, and a drop cap. The editorial language softened with the geometry of the diocesan crest.

01 · Color

Eight brand colors, round chips.

Colors are identical to the editorial system — the curves direction keeps every brand hex. The only change is the shape of the chip: circles echo the jewel bosses on the crest's mitre.

02 · Typography

Same family, softer labels.

Type scale is identical to the editorial system — Instrument Serif for display, Instrument Sans for interface. The only difference: small labels and eyebrows ride inside rounded pill chips instead of flanking hairlines.

05 · Circular numerals

Numbered in discs.

Every list numeral in the system becomes a filled circle. Blue is the default for sequence markers (homepage index, seven sacraments); gold is reserved for the sacrament count inside the navy band; brick is used sparingly for alert or urgent callouts.

I II III IV V VI VII 1 2 3 !

Large 52px blue discs for homepage · 30px gold discs for sacraments on navy · 36px smaller scale for inline sequences

06 · Ornamental flourishes

Church flourishes, set between sections.

Gentle liturgical ornaments mark the breaks between major sections. The primary motif is a small Latin cross flanked by thin gold rules — the most widely-read Catholic mark. Secondary motifs keep the same proportions: the Chi-Rho, the IHS monogram, and the Alpha · Omega pair.

07 · Cards

Rounded corners, soft shadows.

Cards take 16–24px corner radii. A subtle shadow appears on hover. Interior padding stays the same as the editorial system; only the edge softens.

08 · Photography

Arch-topped, softly framed.

Two photo treatments carry the curve language: a rounded rectangle (24px radius) for section photos, and an arch-topped frame for portraits — a gothic cathedral-window shape, with a gold disc peeking from behind.

Arch-topped portrait.

Used only on formal portraits (bishop, clergy). border-radius: 50% 50% 24px 24px / 30% 30% 4% 4% creates the gothic arch silhouette. A 88px bright-gold disc behind the lower-right corner adds the jewel.

10 · Drop cap

Large blue initial.

Long-form intros take an 84px blue serif drop cap on the first letter. No background fill — the letter itself is the accent.

The Catechism calls the sacraments "efficacious signs of grace, instituted by Christ and entrusted to the Church." They are not symbols of something absent but encounters with Someone present.

11 · Accessibility

Curves don't change the contrast.

Every text-on-background pairing hits WCAG AAA at body sizes. Rounded corners and circular chips inherit the same color rules as the editorial system. Focus ring: 2px bright-gold outline, 3px offset.
