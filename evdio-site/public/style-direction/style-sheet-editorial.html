Style sheet · Editorial

The editorial system.

Sharp corners, full-width bands, thin rules, centered kickers with hairlines. The language of a broadsheet paper, dialed down to the scale of a diocesan website.

01 · Color

Eight brand colors, no others.

Every surface draws from these eight colors and their opacities. Navy is the institutional anchor. Teal and blue carry the "blue" family through bands and accents. Brick drives every call to action. Gold flags sacred moments; cream warms the page.

02 · Typography

One family, two registers.

Instrument Serif carries every display and accent moment. Instrument Sans handles everything interactive — buttons, labels, body copy, nav. Italic in a contrasting color is the only decorative move the system needs.

03 · Buttons & CTAs

Sharp corners, sentence case.

All buttons are rectangular. Sentence-case labels, 15px weight 500, arrow slides right on hover. Brick is the single primary color; navy is the outline. Inline text links ride on a dark-gold hairline.

Find a Mass time → Find a parish → Becoming Catholic →

04 · Labels & eyebrows

Three label forms.

Eyebrows frame a headline. Three treatments: centered with hairlines on both sides for hero moments, leading hairline for section intros, top-rule kicker with number for editorial sections.

05 · Cards

Square corners, thin borders.

Story cards, action cards, directory cards all share a sharp-cornered rectangle with a 1px line in navy-at-12% and interior padding of 28–48px. No shadows; the system relies on rules and color bands for hierarchy.

06 · Photography

Real photographs, navy captions.

Only real diocesan photography — no stock, no AI. Hero images fill their column edge-to-edge. Caption bands are navy with cream tracked-caps and a bright-gold highlight word.

07 · Accents

One gesture only: italic in color.

The editorial system adds almost no ornament. The only consistent decorative move is italic accent words inside headlines — blue on hero titles, teal on section titles, brick on kickers. That's enough.

08 · Accessibility

Built to AAA.

Every text-on-background pairing hits WCAG AAA at body sizes. Decorative gold appears only on thin rules and italic heading accents where size and weight grant AA Large compliance. Focus states are a 2px bright-gold outline with 3px offset on every interactive element.
