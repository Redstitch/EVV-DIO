Faith & Sacramentsthe grace-filled life

The Church hands us seven sacred signs — visible moments of invisible grace that carry us from the waters of Baptism to the bedside of anointing.

✠   ❦   ✠

To be Catholic is to believe that God meets us in matter — in water and oil, in bread and wine, in a whispered confession, a wedding vow, the laying on of hands. These are not mere rituals. They are the very action of Christ, still unfolding today in forty-six parishes across Southwestern Indiana.

Whether you're preparing a child for First Communion, standing before Confirmation, planning a Catholic wedding, or walking with a loved one toward the final anointing — this is the doorway. Find what you need, find who can help.

How to begin

Becoming Catholic, or returning after years away.

I

Attend a parish — any parish

You don't need an appointment. You don't need to belong yet. Show up to Mass, sit in the back if you'd like, and stay a moment afterward. The Mass itself is the starting line.

II

Speak with a priest or parish staff

Every parish has someone whose job is to welcome seekers and returners. They'll listen, answer your questions honestly, and help you understand what the next step might look like for your life.

III

Join the OCIA (formerly RCIA) process

The Order of Christian Initiation of Adults walks you through what Catholics believe and practice. Most parishes begin a new group each fall, with sacraments received at Easter.

IV

Receive the sacraments of initiation

At the Easter Vigil, the most ancient liturgy of the year, the Church receives new members through Baptism, Confirmation, and First Communion — or completes what is already begun.
