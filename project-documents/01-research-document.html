The Diocese serves a broad and diverse audience. The website must balance institutional requirements with user-friendly access for people at vastly different stages of their faith journey and familiarity with the Catholic Church.

6. Problems Identified in Discovery

During the introductory meeting on February 9, 2026, the following critical issues were identified through a collaborative walkthrough of both current websites:

Navigation & Structure

  • Department-centric organization: The site is organized around the Diocese's internal structure. Visitors don't think in terms of departments; they think about what they need.
  • Overwhelming menus: Too many items in the navigation. A newcomer looking to get married in the Church, or a family that just moved to Evansville, has no clear starting point.

Storytelling & Impact

  • Invisible impact: The Diocese is doing excellent ministry work and creating compelling video content, but this impact is not visible on the website.
  • Giving without context: The donate button links off-site with no stories of impact, no reason for a visitor to believe their gift will matter.

Content Fragmentation

  • Two separate websites: The Message (the Diocese's own journalism) lives on a completely separate site. This content should be working much harder for the main web presence.
  • Inconsistent maintenance: Of 16 department heads with page management access, only a handful update their content regularly.
  • No single owner: There is no designated website quality owner for the overall experience.

Technical Gaps

  • No site search: Required content (Safe Environment disclosures, allegation information) must be on the site but has no way to be surfaced without prominent placement.
  • Image optimization issues: Very large image files; team has been manually optimizing before upload.
  • Limited mobile experience: Basic responsive design with a single breakpoint.

Primary Reference: Archdiocese of Denver (archden.org)

Identified by the Diocese and Redstitch as the design benchmark during discovery.

Featurearchden.org ApproachApplication to Evansville
Hero Section Full-width image slider with rotating content featuring Archbishop and institutional updates Diocese liked the live hero background. Bishop's face should be prominent (higher up than prototype had it)
Navigation Clean horizontal menu: About, Resources, Schools, Contact, Giving. Quick-access icons for Parish Locator, Clergy Search, Schools Model for simplifying from 16+ items to 6-7 visitor-centric categories
Call-to-Action Cards Four icon-driven cards directing to parishes, clergy, schools, offices Strong pattern for making key actions immediately accessible
Content Feed Card-based grid of press releases and statements Perfect model for integrating The Message articles on the homepage
Spanish Content Spanish-language content available Full-site Spanish translation is a confirmed feature
Newsletter Signup Prominent form for audience engagement Email strategy flagged as future enhancement; design should accommodate

Key Takeaways from Prototype Walkthrough

  • Diocese liked the Bishop's face on the site but wanted it higher up on the page
  • Liked the live hero background from the Denver website
  • Need a dedicated place for the monthly dashboards to live
  • Site search must be prominent for surfacing required but non-featured content

13. Open Items & Outstanding Questions

Needs Internal Resolution Before Build

  • Article publishing process — What does this look like technically in the headless CMS?
  • Parish data ownership — Who maintains parish data, Mass times, contact info, and staff listings? How often does it change?
  • Event registration flow — What happens after someone registers? Where does data go? Can all registrations flow through OSV?
  • Department content model — Will departments still manage their own pages? Or will content be centralized?

Content & Legal Questions

  • What on the current site is legally or institutionally required?
  • What are the absolute non-negotiables?
  • What documents can be archived or removed?
  • Safe Environment — What specifically must be public vs. behind a login?
  • Message archive — What happens to pre-2019 content on evdiomessage-archive.org?

Features & Functionality

  • Spanish translation — Full translation or targeted approach?
  • Sarto Retreat Center & Meeting Rooms — Booking/reservation needed? (Currently out of scope)
  • Clergy/staff directories — Use case for digital directory beyond the Yearbook PDF?
  • Employment — Are school and diocesan listings managed separately? Is the application process digital?
  • Email marketing — Is there a newsletter platform to integrate with? Flocknote mentioned.

Technical & Platform

  • Where do all videos live today — YouTube, Facebook, Vimeo?
  • What about MyDio (Google Sites portal) — What lives there and who uses it?
  • Does the website need to be a resource hub for parishes and staff — forms, policies, internal documents?
  • If so, is there an inventory of these documents and where they need to live?
